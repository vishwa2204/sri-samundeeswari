Frequently Asked Questions

Engineering work fabrication involves the process of creating structures, components, or products through the manipulation of raw materials using various techniques and technologies to meet specific design requirements.

Common materials include metals (such as steel, aluminum, and copper), plastics, composites, and occasionally wood, depending on the application and desired properties.

The fabrication process typically includes design, material selection, cutting, shaping, welding or joining, assembly, and finishing.

Precision is crucial in fabrication work as it directly impacts the performance, safety, and reliability of the final product. Accurate measurements and adherence to design specifications are essential.

Common welding techniques include MIG (Metal Inert Gas), TIG (Tungsten Inert Gas), and stick welding. The choice depends on the materials being joined and the specific requirements of the project.
